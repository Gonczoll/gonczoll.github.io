Essay:The Chaotic World of Lebbeus Woods

When we speak of ?modernism in architecture?, we tend to think of the materiality of Le Corbusier?s purist villas or the simple geometry of Louis Kahn?s projects. However, we do not necessarily think of the vividly colourful and geometrically intricate proposals displayed on the cover page of this essay. Yet, the man behind these drawings, American visionary artist and self-proclaimed architect Lebbeus Woods, was born straight into the golden age of Corbusier and van der Rohe and operated throughout the entirety of what we today refer to as ?late or high modernism?.
